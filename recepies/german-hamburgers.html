German Hamburgers

Frikadellen, the original hamburger, are served like a flattened meatball with string beans and salad. I also like to use this recipe to make Swedish meatballs (but omit the paprika). My aunt in Hamburg, Germany, taught me this recipe, and I've been making it ever since. Serve with steamed string beans and tossed salad. Or serve on a kaiser roll with lettuce, tomato, pickled cucumbers, finely sliced deep-fried onions, and curry ketchup.

Ingredients You'll Need

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 slice white bread, crusts removed
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

How to Make German Hamburgers Step-By-Step

  1. Soak the bread in the milk for a few minutes. Squeeze out excess milk and crumble the bread into a large bowl.
  2. Add the ground beef, ground pork, chopped onion, egg, salt, pepper, paprika, allspice, and nutmeg to the bowl. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into 8 to 10 oval patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the patties and cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are browned and cooked through.
